ABOUT US: MORE THAN
JUST A PODCAST

The Crave is a podcast dedicated to expressing the voice of
young people. Each episode is produced and packaged in an
enthralling, captivating, creative and yet critical way
addressing socio-economic agendas affecting the Youth.

The Crave derived from the iterate word - crave, which
means a strong desire and want for something. it expresses
the immense eagerness for which the Youth have to
experience socio-economic change, It also describes the
facets that the podcast stands for in acronym. Credible/
creative, Reliable, Authoritative, Verifiableand Entertaining.

Hosted by two emancipated minds, The Crave is more than a
podcast. It is a movement in the making. with new guest in
every episode tackling key issues surrounding Mental-Health,
Leadershipand Climate Actioninline withSDG 3,16 and 13,
there is something for everyone. Indeed it is an exceptional,
out of the book way to heal you and the planet.

VISION & MISSION

Vision

To be on the front in pushing for leadership,
Mental Health and Climate Change Conservation.


Mission

To empower the youth to cultivate sustainable
leadership by creating platforms for Mental-Health
and Climate Action Conversations.

OUR WHAT: MESSAGE

Leadership

We recognize that young people
must be the primary drivers of their
own development, with adults and
systems playing a supporting role,
we understand that anything done
for youth without youth runs the risk
of failure. Hence the need to drive
mindful leadership Conversations.

Climate Action

Young people are not only victims of
climate change. They are also
valuable contributors to climate
action. Theyare agents of change,
entrepreneurs and innovators. There
is therefor a need to call them to
action, through tabling Climate
Action Conversations.

Mental Health

Mental health is just as essential as physical health.
Normalizing the conversation about mental health
empowers youth people to talk and get the help they need.
They in turn develop the resilience to cope with whatever life
throws at them and grow into well-rounded, healthy adults.
